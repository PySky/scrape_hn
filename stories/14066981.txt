Later this month, HBO will air a movie starring Oprah Winfrey about the story of Henrietta Lacks, an African-American woman who died of cervical cancer in 1951 but whose cells live on today in laboratories around the world. The film, based on Rebecca Skloot’s best-selling book “The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks,” explores the Lacks family’s struggle to get recognition for the crucial contribution that the Maryland woman inadvertently made to science.
