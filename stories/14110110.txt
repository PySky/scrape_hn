The BBC should face an official investigation into mumbling on prime-time dramas amid concerns that the shows have become "impossible" to hear, peers have said.

Members of the House of Lords highlighted a series of high profile BBC dramas which have attracted complaints including Jamaica Inn, Happy Valley, Invisible Man, Taboo and SS-GB.

Lord Blunkett, the former Home Secretary who is blind, urged the Government to "lean on" Ofcom, the regulator", to ensure that "common sense" prevails.|||
